My Story

My childhood wasn't like most children's. I was enrolled in an Islamic school where seven hours a day were spent purely on religious study, and only one hour on regular schooling. Discipline there was severe — even a small lapse brought harsh punishment.

One day I was struck a hundred times with a stick. The fear was so constant that when I once needed the washroom, I couldn't bring myself to ask the teacher — and had to let it happen where I stood.

Home offered no relief either. I was punished for the smallest things, and there was little effort to improve our situation. That environment wore me down from the inside.

By the time I reached 8th grade, I had started skipping school — and was put to work instead. From morning until night, I worked at a tandoor, at an age when other children were still studying. I've dealt with anemia and physical weakness since childhood; the labor has been hard enough that people often guess I'm in my late thirties, not twenty-two. No close friends, no home of my own, no relative to lean on. I've largely been on my own.

But I never stopped trying to learn. Whenever I got the chance, I taught myself online — AI, programming, engineering. Today I've learned web development, AI integration, and software building, entirely without a formal degree.
