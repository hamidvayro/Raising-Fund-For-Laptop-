My Story

I was born into a very poor family. Life has been full of hardships, but I never gave up.

I started learning on my own with my mobile phone — I taught myself coding, then AI, and kept improving my skills whenever I found the time.

But a laptop is the one thing I need the most now, to move forward and properly build my future.

My childhood wasn't like most children's. I was enrolled in an Islamic school where seven hours a day went purely to religious study, and only one hour to regular schooling. Discipline there was severe — even a small lapse brought harsh punishment, and fear was a constant part of every day.

Home offered little relief either. Money was always tight, and there was rarely room to think beyond getting through each day. That environment wore me down from the inside — but it also taught me how to endure.

By the time I reached 8th grade, I had started skipping school — and was put to work instead. From morning until night, I worked at a tandoor, at an age when other children were still studying. I've dealt with anemia and physical weakness since childhood, and the years of hard labor have taken a visible toll — people often guess I'm in my late thirties, not twenty-two. No close friends, no home of my own, no relative to lean on. I've largely been on my own.

But I never stopped trying to learn. Whenever I got the chance, I taught myself online — AI, programming, engineering — on nothing more than a mobile phone. Today, I want to go further: to properly learn web development, AI integration, and software building, and use those skills to build a better life for myself and my family.
